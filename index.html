2

Frequently asked questions

Is Tubify free?

Yes — Tubify is completely free, with no ads, no subscriptions and no account required.

Which sites does Tubify support?

Tubify downloads from YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, Facebook, X (Twitter) and over 1,000 other video and music sites.

Can I download YouTube videos as MP3?

Yes. You can save any video as audio in MP3 or M4A, or download the video itself in any resolution up to 4K.

Is Tubify safe to install?

Yes. Tubify is a clean Android app. Because it isn't on the Play Store, Android may show a Play Protect prompt for new apps — just tap More details, then Install anyway.

Do I need an account?

No. There's no sign-up or login — install the app and start downloading right away.

How do I update Tubify?

Download the latest app from this page and install it over your current version — your downloads and settings are kept.

How do I download a video with Tubify?

Open Tubify, paste a link or search, pick the format and quality you want, then tap download. The file is saved to your device.

Where are my downloads saved?

By default in your phone's Download/Tubify folder. You can choose any folder in Settings → Save location.

Can I download videos in 4K or 1080p?

Yes. Before downloading you can pick the exact resolution a video offers, from 360p up to 4K.

Can I download TikTok or Instagram videos without a watermark?

Tubify saves TikTok and Instagram videos in their original form. When a watermark-free source is available, that is the version you get.

Is there a download limit?

No. There is no daily cap, no queue limit and no file-size limit — download as much as you like.

Does Tubify work on iPhone (iOS)?

No. Tubify is an Android app and there is no iOS version.

Is it legal to use Tubify?

Tubify is a general-purpose download tool. Only download content you own or have permission to save, and respect the rights of creators and each site's terms of service.

Why isn't Tubify on the Google Play Store?

Google Play doesn't allow apps that download videos from YouTube, so Tubify is distributed directly from this site. That's also why Android may show a one-time Play Protect prompt the first time you install.
